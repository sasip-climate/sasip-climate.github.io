Scaling Simulations of Local Wind-Waves Amid Sea Ice Floes

Wave-ice interactions are crucial for modeling air-sea exchanges in polar regions. While sea ice’s impact on open-water swell is well understood, its effect on locally generated wind-waves in partial ice cover is uncertain. In this new paper published in JGR Oceans, Samuel Brenner and Christopher Horvat use the SWAN model to show that wave growth depends on both sea ice concentration (SIC) and floe size distribution (FSD). The results suggest that these waves, influenced by fetch limitations, can significantly affect ocean mixing.

Figure 1:Schematic showing the growth of (a) significant wave height, , and (b) peak wave period , with open water distance (fetch, ) between sea ice floes of various sizes (; shown in gray) in a one-dimensional domain with wind blowing from left to right. Wave statistics in the schematic were generated using the wave model SWAN (Section 2.4), run in one-dimensional mode in a similar configuration to that used in the remainder of the study.

Self-attentive Transformer for Fast and Accurate Postprocessing of Temperature and Wind Speed Forecasts

In this preprint submitted to Artificial Intelligence for the Earth Systems, Aaron Van Poecke, Tobias Sebastian Finn et al. advance the ensemble transformer, a deep learning model to post-process and calibrate ensemble forecasts from NWP, and show that it outperforms classical more-often used methods while being up to 75 times faster.
